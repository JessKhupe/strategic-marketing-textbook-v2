# Chapter 2: AI & Machine Learning Foundations for Marketers

!!! note "Bloom's Level & Duration"
    **Bloom's Level:** Understand, Apply  
    **Duration:** 2 weeks

## Summary

This chapter demystifies AI and machine learning for non-technical marketing professionals. Students learn to distinguish between AI types, understand when each applies to marketing problems, and develop the vocabulary to collaborate with data science teams. The focus is on strategic deployment decisions rather than technical implementation.

## Learning Objectives

1. Differentiate supervised learning (prediction), unsupervised learning (pattern discovery), and reinforcement learning (optimization) with marketing examples.
2. Apply feature engineering thinking to identify which customer data points predict marketing outcomes.
3. Evaluate when to use pre-trained models versus training custom models.
4. Translate AI model outputs into actionable marketing decisions.

## Core Theory

- Supervised vs unsupervised vs reinforcement learning
- Neural networks and deep learning basics
- Natural Language Processing (NLP) for marketing
- Computer vision applications (UGC, ad creative analysis)
- Transfer learning and pre-trained models
- Model training, validation, and overfitting
- Bias and variance trade-offs

## Frameworks

| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Marketing AI Readiness Ladder | Assess organizational readiness across data, people, tooling, and governance |
| Build vs Buy vs Partner Matrix | Decide whether to build custom models, buy SaaS, or partner for AI solutions |
| Data Quality Pyramid | Prioritize data improvements (completeness → correctness → accessibility) |

## AI Tool Applications

| Tool | Use case |
|---|---|
| ChatGPT / Claude | Content ideation, summarization, campaign copy drafts |
| Google Vertex AI | Custom model training and deployment at scale |
| HuggingFace | Pre-trained NLP models for classification, summarization |
| DataRobot | Automated machine learning workflows for rapid prototyping |

## Ethical Checkpoints

!!! warning "Ethical Checkpoints"
    - **Algorithmic bias in training data:** Audit datasets for skewed representation.  
    - **Black box decisions affecting customers:** Favor explainability for high stakes outputs.  
    - **Data privacy in model training:** Ensure data law compliance and minimization.  
    - **Accountability:** Define owners for model decisions and remediation processes.

## MicroSim: The Model Selection Challenge

Students are given five marketing problems (churn prediction, content generation, image recognition for UGC, price optimization, sentiment analysis). For each problem they must:

- Select the appropriate AI approach (supervised/unsupervised/reinforcement or generative/transfer learning).  
- Justify their selection with consideration of data availability, latency, explainability, and ethical risk.
