# Chapter 6: Customer Analytics: From Hindsight to Foresight

!!! note "Bloom's Level & Duration"
    **Bloom's Level:** Analyze, Evaluate  
    **Duration:** 2 weeks

## Summary

This chapter moves students from descriptive analytics to predictive and prescriptive analytics. Students build churn prediction models, CLV forecasting systems, and next‑best‑action engines to turn data into proactive marketing decisions.

## Learning Objectives

1. Build churn prediction models using behavioral signals with 80%+ accuracy.  
2. Design CLV forecasting systems that inform acquisition and retention investment.  
3. Implement cohort analysis frameworks that reveal product‑market fit signals.  
4. Create customer health scoring systems for proactive intervention.

## Core Theory

- Descriptive vs predictive vs prescriptive analytics
- Cohort analysis
- RFM modeling (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)
- Churn prediction and survival analysis
- Customer health scoring and next‑best‑action modeling
- Causal inference vs correlation

## Frameworks

| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Analytics Maturity Ladder | Roadmap from descriptive dashboards to prescriptive automation |
| Predictive Signal Hierarchy | Rank signals by predictive power for outcomes like churn or conversion |
| Intervention ROI Framework | Prioritize interventions by expected impact and cost |

## AI Tool Applications

| Tool | Use case |
|---|---|
| Mixpanel / Amplitude | Behavioral analytics and funnel tracking |
| Python / R | Custom modeling (survival analysis, forecasting) |
| Gainsight / Salesforce Einstein | Customer health scoring and predictive alerts |

## Ethical Checkpoints

!!! warning "Ethical Checkpoints"
    - **Predictive targeting of vulnerable customers:** Avoid exploiting vulnerability identified by models.  
    - **Acting on predictions prematurely:** Validate models before automated action.  
    - **Data determinism:** Preserve human agency and avoid deterministic treatment plans.

## MicroSim: The Churn War Room

Students receive 90 days of behavioral data, identify at‑risk segments, predict churn probability, design interventions, and measure ROI of retention spend.
