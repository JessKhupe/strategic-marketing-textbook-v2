# Chapter 9: Ethical AI Marketing & Responsible Automation

!!! note "Bloom's Level & Duration"
    **Bloom's Level:** Evaluate, Create  
    **Duration:** 2 weeks

## Summary

This chapter treats ethical AI as strategic advantage. Students build governance frameworks, bias auditing processes, and transparency systems to maintain trust while preserving performance.

## Learning Objectives

1. Audit AI marketing systems for bias using fairness metrics.  
2. Design governance frameworks balancing innovation with risk management.  
3. Build explainability systems for customer‑facing AI decisions.  
4. Evaluate regulatory landscape and compliance requirements.

## Core Theory

- Algorithmic fairness and bias types
- Demographic parity, equalized odds
- Explainable AI (XAI) fundamentals
- AI governance frameworks and continuous review
- Dark patterns and UX ethics

## Frameworks

| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Ethical AI Audit Framework | Structured audit steps to detect and remediate bias |
| Fairness Metrics Dashboard | Operationalize parity and fairness metrics |
| AI Governance Maturity Model | Roadmap for policies, roles, and processes |

## AI Tool Applications

| Tool | Use case |
|---|---|

| SHAP / LIME | Explainability for model predictions |

## Ethical Checkpoints

!!! warning "Ethical Checkpoints"
    - **Bias in training data:** Monitor and correct for historical biases.  
    - **Manipulative personalization:** Draw clear ethical boundaries.  
    - **Environmental costs:** Consider compute impacts of large models.

## MicroSim: The Ethics Crisis Simulation

Students diagnose a bias discovered in a recommendation engine, quantify impact, fix the model, and design monitoring and communication plans.
