# Chapter 7: Attribution & Performance Measurement

!!! note "Bloom's Level & Duration"
    **Bloom's Level:** Analyze, Evaluate  
    **Duration:** 2 weeks

## Summary

This chapter tackles measuring which activities drive revenue across complex customer journeys. Students move beyond last‑click attribution to build multi‑touch and data‑driven measurement frameworks informing budget allocation.

## Learning Objectives

1. Compare attribution models and their strategic implications.  
2. Design multi‑touch attribution systems for long B2B sales cycles.  
3. Build marketing mix modeling frameworks for channel investment decisions.  
4. Implement incrementality testing to validate attribution outputs.

## Core Theory

- Attribution model types and trade‑offs
- Multi‑touch attribution and MMM
- Incrementality testing and uplift measurement
- Cross‑device and offline‑to‑online attribution

## Frameworks

| Framework | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Attribution Ladder | Progression from simple to sophisticated attribution approaches |
| Budget Allocation Decision Matrix | Formalize tradeoffs across ROI, brand impact, and strategic goals |
| Incrementality Testing Protocol | Steps to design holdout and experiment tests for causal measurement |

## Ethical Checkpoints

!!! warning "Ethical Checkpoints"
    - **Undervaluing channels:** Beware models that systematically ignore brand or future value.  
    - **Privacy implications:** Cross‑device tracking has privacy and consent challenges.  
    - **Overfitting measurement to short‑term metrics:** Keep long‑term brand health in view.

## MicroSim: The Attribution Model Architect

Students manage B2B SaaS marketing spend across multiple channels and build attribution models using simulated multi‑touch journey data, then validate against revenue outcomes.
